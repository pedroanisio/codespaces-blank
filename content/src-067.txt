The 1990s was a decade of major Hollywood blockbusters. Many longstanding franchises were born in the '90s and many more saw their greatest successes on the big screen. These sought-after films resulted in massive ticket sales at the box office, as well as widespread VHS and/or DVD sales.

Companies such as Disney, LucasFilm, and Paramount made a huge splash with films that had high production values, compelling stories, and plenty of star power. There were countless iconic films made in the '90s. In terms of gross profit, however, each year of the decade could only have one winner.

10 Home Alone Took The Top Box Office Spot In 1990 ($285,761,243 Gross)
 Image via 20th Century Studios 

The first year of the '90s saw the birth of the Home Alone franchise, which had five theatrical releases in total. The basic premise of the franchise would be pretty harrowing to experience in real life, but the movies are comedic gems - especially the first one.

Macaulay Culkin stars as Kevin McCallister, a young boy who is accidentally left home alone while his family sets off on a Christmas vacation to Paris, France. Unfortunately, Kevin soon finds himself at odds with two burglars and is tasked with saving his family home from the bumbling intruders. Overall, Home Alone made $285,761,243.

9 Terminator 2: Judgment Day Outshined The Competition In 1991 ($204,843,345 Gross)

Terminator 2: Judgment Day is arguably the best of the Terminator sequels, which explains its stellar performance when it was released in 1991. Judgment Day has so many elements that fans loved: a high-energy Guns N' Roses soundtrack, impressive CGI for the time, and the return of Arnold Schwarzenegger.

RELATED: 10 Must-See Movies From The 1990s That Still Hold Up

Judgment Day puts a new spin on the original story, turning Schwarzenegger's character into the film's hero instead of its villain. James Cameron took the franchise in such a novel direction that this film remains a nostalgic favorite even thirty years later. Overall, Judgment Day made $204,843,345.

8 Aladdin Was A Huge Hit For Disney In 1992 ($217,350,219 Gross)

Disney had many theatrical hits in the 1990s, but Aladdin was a top performer. This was largely due to the star-studded voice cast for the film, including the late Robin Williams as Genie and the late Gilbert Gottfried as the evil macaw, Iago.

Both comedians-turned-actors were at the top of their game in the '90s, and their participation in Aladdin was a big draw for audiences. Colorful animation and a memorable soundtrack also made the film an instant favorite for fans of all ages. It managed to earn $217,350,219.

7 Jurassic Park Gained A Massive Following In 1993 ($357,067,947 Gross)

Though the final Jurassic Park movie hit theaters in 2022, fans are still enthralled by the original Steven Spielberg film from 1993. The franchise is actually an adaptation of Michael Crichton's 1990 book of the same name.

The special effects in Jurassic Park were very impressive for the time, and the fast-moving plot kept viewers on the edge of their seats. Jeff Goldblum and Sam Neil headline a lovable cast and take viewers through the fascinating eponymous park until - in true Hollywood fashion - everything goes horribly, dramatically wrong.

6 Forrest Gump Won Over Fans In 1994 ($329,694,499 Gross)

Forrest Gump follows one man through several decades of his unassumingly fascinating life. The film is based on a novel by Winston Groom and stars Tom Hanks in one of his most iconic roles to date. The saga of Forrest's life spans the '60s, '70s, and '80s, some of the most iconic periods in 20th century American history.

Forrest finds himself at the heart of multiple historical events, but seems unaware of the significance of these events as they're occurring. His innocence is part of what makes him a lovable character for most audiences. Forrest Gump also runs the gamut of emotions: viewers will laugh, gasp, and cry at different memorable moments along the way. These elements ultimately earned the film $329,694,499.

5 Toy Story Kick-Started A Pixar Franchise In 1995 ($191,796,233 Gross)

When Pixar was still independently-owned, it launched a movie franchise about lovable childhood toys that went on adventures together. The first Toy Story movie premiered in 1995, and every '90s kid was clamoring to go see it in theaters.

Tom Hanks and Tim Allen lent their voices to the most popular main characters: Woody and Buzz Lightyear. The franchise has spawned four movies, toys, video games, and a series of graphic novels.

4 Independence Day Topped The Box Office List In 1996 ($306,169,268 Gross)

Will Smith headlined several hit movies in the '90s, but Independence Day was among the most successful. Independence Day was like many action films of the decade: fast-paced, packed with special effects, and backed by the star power of its cast. The film was also popular because of its alien invasion theme, which was a common sight in sci-fi films at the time.

What's more, Independence Day had an undeniable sense of patriotism; Americans had to unite against the common alien enemy in order to save themselves and the world. The feel-good ending inspired many to watch the movie again. Independence Day earned $306,169,268.

3 Titanic Set Records In 1997 ($600,788,188 Gross)

James Cameron struck gold with this moving, tragic film in 1997. Titanic starred Leonardo DiCaprio and Kate Winslet, and depicted a dramatized version of the harrowing historical events that led to the sinking of the iconic cruise liner in the 1920s. Titanic became the highest-grossing film of all time, and held onto that top spot until 2009.

Ironically, Titanic was replaced by Avatar, another James Cameron film. Clearly, Cameron knows how to draw a large audience with his films. To this day, Titanic is still the third highest-grossing film ever, even outshining films from some of the most popular franchises in Hollywood.

2 Saving Private Ryan Filled Theater Seats In 1988 ($216,540,909 Gross)

As far as war epics go, Saving Private Ryan is a standout. The film was directed by Steven Spielberg and takes place around the time of the Normandy Landings in WWII. Saving Private Ryan pulls no punches, graphically depicting the atrocities of war like few films before it.

Audiences couldn't pull their eyes away from the action as Captain John H. Miller (Tom Hanks) leads his troops behind enemy lines to rescue one of their own. Saving Private Ryan is absolutely stunning in its brutality and its humanity, and is endlessly re-watchable. Ultimately, it earned $216,540,909.

1 Star Wars: Episode 1 - The Phantom Menace Ushered In A New Star Wars Era In 1999 ($431,088,295 Gross)

The Star Wars franchise is among the most well-known American film franchises in history, and it shows no signs of slowing down. Episode VI: Return of the Jedi was released in 1983, and there was a sixteen-year pause before Episode 1: The Phantom Menace made its debut.

The Phantom Menace was designed to be a prequel to the three pre-existing Star Wars films, and combined some of the franchise's iconic characters with some fresh new faces. The film's box office performance demonstrated that Star Wars fans were definitely ready for more, and the franchise has been churning out films and television series steadily ever since.
